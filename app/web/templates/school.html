NYC DOE budget categories use a few opaque acronyms:

  • OTPS — Other Than Personal Services: everything that isn't salaries — supplies, contracts, software, per-pupil licenses, instructional materials.
  • SBST — School-Based Support Team: staff (psychologist, social worker, special-ed evaluator) who assess students for special-ed services and develop IEPs.
  • Per Diem — daily pay for substitute teachers and other temporary staff.
  • Per Session — After/Before School, — Professional Development, — Miscellaneous — extra hourly pay for teachers working outside their regular schedule (tutoring, training, club coaching, etc.).
  • Paraprofessionals — classroom aides, often supporting students with IEPs in ICT or self-contained classrooms.
  • School Site — facilities/maintenance allocation for the building itself.
  • Special Needs - Support Services — services beyond a teacher's classroom (speech, OT, counseling) for students with IEPs.

NYS {{ term("ESSA", "Every Student Succeeds Act — the federal education accountability law (2015) that replaced No Child Left Behind. NY computes designations under it annually.") }} accountability

{{ provenance( ["nysed-src-2025-accountability-status.feather", "nysed-src-2025-accountability-status-by-subgroup.feather"], "NYSED School Report Card Database (April 2026 release). Status reflects the next school year's designation: a 2025 row is the 2025-26 designation based on 2024-25 results. ESSA = the federal Every Student Succeeds Act (2015), which replaced No Child Left Behind. It tells each state to identify schools needing extra support based on multiple performance indicators — test scores, growth, English-learner progress, attendance, graduation rate. NYSED computes the designation annually for every NY public school.

Four tiers, best to worst:

  • Local Support and Improvement (LSI) — the default, good-standing tier. The school is meeting its accountability targets across all indicators and demographic subgroups. No federal flag. The name sounds ominous but it just means the school handles its own continuous improvement locally, with no state or federal intervention required. ~80%+ of NY schools sit here.
  • Targeted Support and Improvement (TSI) — at least one demographic subgroup (students with disabilities, English-language learners, a racial group, etc.) is performing in the bottom tier. The school must write a subgroup-focused improvement plan.
  • Additional Targeted Support and Improvement (ATSI) — a TSI school where the flagged subgroup's performance is so low it would qualify as Comprehensive if it were the whole school. Stronger intervention required.
  • Comprehensive Support and Improvement (CSI) — the lowest tier. Triggered by being in the bottom 5% of Title I schools statewide, having a 4-year graduation rate ≤ 67% for two consecutive years, or being ATSI for three years without improvement. State-level support plus a mandatory improvement plan.

Year column: the 2024-25 row is the designation that applies during the 2024-25 school year, computed from 2023-24 results. The 2025-26 row is next year's designation, from 2024-25 results. NYSED publishes the designation about a year ahead so families and schools have notice.

When a school is flagged, the row is rendered in amber with a "flagged" pill, and a separate "Subgroups flagged" callout below lists which subgroups triggered TSI/ATSI.
